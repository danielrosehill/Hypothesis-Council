The US has gotten cold feet about the Iran operation, or Trump has finally acknowledged that Iran recovering the enriched uranium is viable (i.e. the strikes did not finish the job). The snap ceasefire deal with Lebanon is a ruse: its real objective is to shift the burden of maintaining the ceasefire away from the US and onto Israel. The fighting over the Strait of Hormuz is therefore mostly performative. Lebanon has performatively committed to a ceasefire, vowing to rid the country of Hezbollah. The goal is to deflect responsibility for any escalation squarely onto Iran.

In other words: the US and Iran have engineered a mutually agreeable off-ramp by which it falls to Israel to take on the herculean mission of finishing the war objectives unilaterally. Iran has the Lebanon ceasefire and the scope ambiguity as its ready-made excuse for resuming the war when it wants to, and can escalate on that front to signal readiness for confrontation. Israel can use the same mechanism to probe Iran.

Meanwhile, both sides are urgently readying for round two. Israel may be using civilian readiness — and the relaxing of wartime restrictions — as disinformation. Iran may be using the Strait of Hormuz tensions for a similar purpose: to distract attention from what it is actually focusing on.

Prediction: the Lebanon ceasefire will unravel the moment it ceases to serve its purpose — which will be very soon — and full war will resume during or shortly before Yom Ha'atzmaut.
